Questions

Does this work with Google Workspace for Nonprofits (the free tier)?

Gemini features in Docs, Sheets, and Gmail require a Gemini add-on or Business/Enterprise tier. However, NotebookLM, AI Studio, and standalone Gemini are free for anyone with a Google account. We'll clearly map what's available at your license level in Week 1, and help you make the case for upgrades if the ROI is there.

What's the difference between Gemini, Gems, and NotebookLM?

Gemini is Google's AI assistant — it lives inside your Workspace apps (Gmail, Docs, Sheets) and helps you write, analyze, and create. Gems are custom AI personas you build on top of Gemini — specialized experts trained for your specific tasks. NotebookLM is a research tool where you upload documents and get answers with citations. AI Studio lets you build and test custom prompts. Vids is Google's AI video creator. They're complementary, and we cover all five.

Will I need admin access to my organization's Google Workspace?

No. Everything we cover works from a regular user account. If your admin has disabled certain AI features, we'll show you how to have that conversation with your IT team — including a template request email.

What about data privacy? Is it safe to use Gemini with sensitive nonprofit data?

Great question — and one we take very seriously. We cover data handling and privacy in every session, and Week 4 specifically addresses governance. For Business and Enterprise plans, Google does not use your data to train its models. We'll help you understand exactly what happens with your data at your tier.

What if I miss a session?

All sessions are recorded and available within 24 hours. You also have weekly office hours to catch up. That said, the live sessions are where the hands-on learning happens — we encourage attending live when possible.

What's the COMPAS framework?

COMPAS is MTM's methodology for responsible AI adoption: Context, Objective, Method, Performance, Assessment, Sharing. It ensures every AI initiative starts with understanding your organization and ends with knowledge transfer. The curriculum is built on COMPAS principles — you won't just learn features, you'll learn how to adopt them sustainably.
